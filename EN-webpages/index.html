Background

As China continues its rise and is becoming a major economic power in the world, the education of Chinese is starting to become global. This mega trend of China global education is manifesting in three areas:

  • 1) Establishment of new international schools in China, by Chinese and largely for Chinese;
  • 2) Chinese students going abroad to study, especially younger students going to international boarding schools for elementary and high school, as well as vocational trainings and universities;
  • 3) International universities establishing campuses in China.

At the same time, global education continues to evolve and expand with new concepts and new technologies. The Americans are championing GRIT education. Mark Zuckerberg, the founder of Facebook, is investing in AltSchool, which emphasizes individual development and personalized education. China’s Zhen Fund recently invested in U.S. based Minerva University, which aims to build a new global, online, and personalized education model that can rival traditional ivy league schools. MOOCs (Massive Online Open Course) are being offered at prestigious schools such as MIT and Stanford. The emergence of Virtual Reality (VR) and Augmented Reality (AR) promises great potential applications for global education.

2016 The inaugural China Global Education Summit and Expo is inviting educational pioneers, technology innovators, investors, students, parents, teachers, and education service providers, both in China and globally, to come together, showcase to each other, learn from each other, share with each other, and grow together, for the advancement of China Global Education.

Attendee Profiles

The Expo has both forum and exhibition. More than 100 elite international schools, boarding schools, colleges, vocational schools, universities from China, US, UK, Europe, Australia, Canada, and Singapore are invited. More than 1,000 teachers, students, parents, school administrators, admission officers, interviewing officers are expected to attend. The exhibiting organizations include schools, education technology providers, and education service providers.
